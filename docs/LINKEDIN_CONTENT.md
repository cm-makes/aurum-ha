## 2. About Section (max. 2000 Zeichen)

AURUM is a free, open-source Home Assistant integration that turns your solar surplus into smart device scheduling.

Instead of exporting excess PV energy to the grid at minimal feed-in rates, AURUM automatically activates household devices — washing machines, dishwashers, pool pumps, EV chargers — when your solar production exceeds consumption.

**Key Features:**
• Priority-based device management with configurable thresholds
• Battery-aware scheduling — respects SOC targets and charging states
• Startup Detection — detects when appliances like washing machines are loaded and waiting
• Price-Aware Scheduling — runs devices on cheap grid electricity (Tibber, Nordpool, aWATTar) when solar isn't available
• Fully configurable via Home Assistant UI — no YAML editing required
• Real-time dashboard with Mushroom Cards

**Why AURUM?**
Most PV owners lose money by exporting cheap and importing expensive. AURUM bridges this gap with intelligent, automated self-consumption optimization that works with any inverter and any smart plug.

Built by a home automation enthusiast, battle-tested on a real household with Fronius inverter, BYD battery, and Shelly smart plugs.

Available on HACS (Home Assistant Community Store).

## 6. Introductory Post #2 – Price-Aware Scheduling Feature

**Titel:** New in AURUM v1.7: Price-Aware Scheduling ⚡

Solar surplus is great during the day — but what about cloudy days and evenings?

AURUM v1.7 introduces Price-Aware Scheduling: connect your electricity price sensor (Tibber, Nordpool, aWATTar, EPEX Spot) and AURUM will automatically run devices when grid power is cheap.

How it works:
→ Set a max price per device (e.g., "run dishwasher below 15 ct/kWh")
→ AURUM reacts instantly when prices drop
→ Devices stop immediately when prices rise
→ Solar surplus always takes priority over cheap grid

Combined with PV surplus optimization, your devices now run at the lowest possible cost — whether that's free solar or cheap grid.

🔗 https://github.com/cm-makes/aurum-ha
